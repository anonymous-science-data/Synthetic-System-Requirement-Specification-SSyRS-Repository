
Retail and Supply-Chain Scenario
1. System Overview
System Purpose:
The system is designed to optimize inventory management, enhance supply chain visibility, and improve customer satisfaction through predictive analytics, real-time tracking, and automated order processing.

Domain/Context:
Retail and supply-chain management, specifically for mid-to-large-scale retailers and their logistics partners.

Stakeholders:

Retailers (store managers, procurement teams).
Suppliers and manufacturers.
Logistics providers.
Customers (end-users benefiting from improved availability and delivery times).
IT administrators and system integrators.
User Base Characteristics:

Size: Thousands of users distributed across retail chains and logistics hubs.
Diversity: Users range from non-technical staff (store clerks) to technical administrators and data analysts.
Geographic Distribution: Spread across multiple regions with variations in language and local regulatory requirements.
Roles: Operational users (inventory managers), decision-makers (supply chain strategists), and system administrators.
2. Functional Requirements
Core Features:

Real-time inventory tracking and updates.
Predictive analytics for demand forecasting.
Automated alerts for restocking and potential delays.
Integration with IoT devices for warehouse automation.
User dashboards for performance metrics and supply chain KPIs.
Vendor management and contract tracking.
Frequency of Authentication:

Daily authentication for general users.
Per-session authentication for critical system functions (e.g., vendor payment approvals).
Sensitivity of Actions:

High sensitivity: Editing vendor contracts, modifying inventory thresholds.
Medium sensitivity: Viewing detailed supply chain analytics.
Low sensitivity: Checking inventory levels.
3. Non-Functional Requirements
Performance:

Sub-second response times for user queries.
Capability to process up to 10,000 simultaneous transactions.
Scalability:

Designed to handle a 300% increase in user base during seasonal peaks.
Flexible architecture to accommodate integration with additional IoT devices.
Reliability:

99.9% uptime with redundancy mechanisms for critical operations.
Automatic failover support during hardware or software failures.
Security:

Role-based access control (RBAC).
End-to-end encryption for sensitive data during transmission and storage.
Regular security audits and compliance checks.
Usability:

Multilingual support for diverse user bases.
Mobile-friendly interfaces for on-the-go access.
Intuitive workflows requiring minimal training.
4. Constraints
Technical Constraints:

Must operate on hybrid cloud infrastructure.
Limited support for legacy systems, requiring gradual migration.
Compliance Requirements:

Adherence to GDPR for user data.
Compliance with local trade regulations and customs reporting standards.
Resource Constraints:

Development limited to a 12-month timeline.
Fixed budget requiring prioritization of features.
Integration Needs:

Seamless integration with existing ERP systems.
APIs to interface with third-party logistics providers and payment gateways.
This scenario highlights a robust system designed to modernize retail and supply-chain operations with an emphasis on efficiency, security, and user experience.
