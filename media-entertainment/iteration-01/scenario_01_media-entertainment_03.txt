Scenario: Media and Entertainment Sector
1. System Overview
System Purpose:
The system, CreativeSync, is designed to streamline content creation, licensing, and monetization in the Media and Entertainment sector. It facilitates collaboration among creators, rights holders, and distributors, providing a unified platform to manage creative assets from production to distribution.

Domain/Context:
The system operates in the Media and Entertainment domain, focusing on digital media production, rights management, and audience engagement.

Stakeholders:

Content Creators: Filmmakers, designers, writers, and musicians producing original works.
Rights Holders: Organizations or individuals managing intellectual property rights.
Distributors: Platforms and networks responsible for publishing and broadcasting content.
Marketing Teams: Professionals leveraging audience insights to drive engagement.
End-Users: Consumers accessing and purchasing media content.
User Base Characteristics:

Size: Approximately 75,000 active users, with growth expected to reach 150,000 in five years.
Diversity: Wide-ranging user roles from creatives to corporate executives.
Geographic Distribution: Global user base with significant clusters in North America, Europe, and Asia-Pacific.
Roles: Includes creators, editors, administrators, marketers, and distributors.
2. Functional Requirements
Core Features:

Asset Management: Centralized repository for creative assets, with version control and metadata tagging.
Collaboration Tools: Real-time editing, review workflows, and task assignment capabilities.
Rights Management: Tools to handle licensing, royalties, and intellectual property tracking.
Content Distribution: Integration with major platforms like Netflix, Hulu, and Spotify for seamless publishing.
Monetization Tools: Analytics to track revenue, manage subscriptions, and offer in-app purchases.
Frequency of Authentication:

Regular Users: Single sign-on (SSO) required at login.
Admins and Sensitive Operations: Multi-factor authentication (MFA) required for administrative actions and rights management tasks.
Sensitivity of Actions:

High Sensitivity: Managing licensing agreements, publishing content, and handling financial transactions.
Medium Sensitivity: Editing metadata, approving workflows, and modifying permissions.
Low Sensitivity: Viewing dashboards and collaborating on drafts.
3. Non-Functional Requirements
Performance:

Sub-2-second response times for most user actions.
Support for up to 15,000 concurrent users during peak usage.
Scalability:

Ability to scale to accommodate 300% growth in user base and asset volume within five years.
Reliability:

99.9% uptime with automated backups and disaster recovery processes.
Error reporting system with automatic notification to administrators.
Security:

Role-based access control (RBAC) to ensure users only access authorized areas.
Encryption for all data in transit and at rest.
Regular security audits and compliance with ISO/IEC 27001 standards.
Usability:

Intuitive user interface tailored for both technical and non-technical users.
Accessibility compliance with WCAG 2.1 standards to ensure inclusivity.
4. Constraints
Technical Constraints:

Operates on a multi-cloud infrastructure to ensure data availability and minimize latency.
Limited support for legacy file formats, requiring conversion tools.
Compliance Requirements:

GDPR compliance for European users.
Adherence to DMCA and global copyright laws.
Accessibility compliance with WCAG 2.1 standards.
Resource Constraints:

Development budget capped at $4 million for the first two years.
Core team limited to 60 developers, designers, and QA specialists.
Project timeline of 18 months for initial deployment.
Integration Needs:

Must integrate with popular creative tools like Adobe Creative Cloud and enterprise systems like SAP for licensing and revenue tracking.
This scenario outlines a detailed and realistic system designed to address the specific needs of the Media and Entertainment sector, focusing on content creation, management, and distribution workflows.
