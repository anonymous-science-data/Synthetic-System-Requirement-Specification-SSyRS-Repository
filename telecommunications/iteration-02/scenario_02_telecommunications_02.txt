
1. System Overview
System Purpose:
To develop an intelligent customer experience management (CEM) system that personalizes user interactions, monitors service quality, and provides real-time insights for improving customer satisfaction across telecommunications services.

Domain/Context:
Telecommunications sector, focusing on customer relationship management, service quality, and user engagement.

Stakeholders:

Telecom Service Providers: Utilize the system to analyze and enhance customer experiences.
Customers: Benefit from personalized support and proactive issue resolution.
Customer Service Teams: Leverage tools to address inquiries efficiently and improve service delivery.
Product Managers: Gain insights into customer behavior and service trends for better product development.
User Base Characteristics:

Size: Hundreds of thousands of users globally.
Diversity: Broad demographic range, spanning different cultures, languages, and usage habits.
Geographic Distribution: Operates in urban, suburban, and rural areas across multiple countries.
Roles: Customers, customer service representatives, system administrators, and analytics teams.
2. Functional Requirements
Core Features:

Automated sentiment analysis from customer feedback and service interactions.
Self-service options for troubleshooting, account management, and service upgrades.
Real-time notifications about service outages and resolution timelines.
AI-driven suggestions for customer support agents to resolve issues faster.
Centralized dashboard displaying customer satisfaction metrics and service KPIs.
Frequency of Authentication:

Customers: Per session for access to self-service features and sensitive account information.
Service Representatives: Per session with additional authentication for accessing sensitive customer data.
System Administrators: Per action for high-sensitivity tasks like system configurations or access control changes.
Sensitivity of Actions:

High sensitivity: Access to customer personal data, service history, and payment details.
Medium sensitivity: Automated service notifications and general account interactions.
3. Non-Functional Requirements
Performance:

Average response times of less than 1 second for customer queries.
Capable of processing up to 1 million sentiment analysis operations per hour.
Scalability:

Seamless expansion to accommodate a 30% annual increase in customer base.
Elastic infrastructure to manage peaks during promotional campaigns or service outages.
Reliability:

99.95% uptime with real-time monitoring of system health.
Instant failover to backup systems during disruptions.
Security:

Role-based access control with multi-factor authentication (MFA).
Compliance with encryption standards (e.g., AES-256) for data storage and transmission.
Continuous monitoring for unauthorized access attempts.
Usability:

Mobile-friendly design to cater to customers accessing services via smartphones.
Localized interfaces supporting multiple languages and currencies.
4. Constraints
Technical Constraints:

Integration with legacy customer databases and billing systems.
Requires deployment on both cloud and on-premises environments.
Compliance Requirements:

Must comply with GDPR for handling European customer data.
Adherence to local telecommunications data retention laws.
Resource Constraints:

Initial project budget capped at $8 million.
Development team limited to 40 personnel, including outsourced resources.
Integration Needs:

Compatibility with existing CRM and contact center software.
Must integrate with AI-driven tools for sentiment analysis and predictive insights.
